Nangkiew Foundation Insights is our space for serious, accessible writing on the questions that shape opportunity in Meghalaya.

We want every article to do more than describe a problem. Our aim is to connect **evidence, institutions and practical pathways** — whether the subject is education, skills, higher education, public finance, technology, employment or regional development.

## What readers can expect

Our articles will generally follow three principles:

- **Evidence before assertion.** Where a claim depends on data, policy or research, we will link readers to the underlying source.
- **Meghalaya in context.** Local questions will be examined alongside lessons from the North East, India and other relevant regions.
- **Ideas that lead somewhere.** Commentary should help readers understand not only what is happening, but what institutions, communities and young people can realistically do next.

> Good public discussion becomes more useful when evidence is understandable, local realities are taken seriously, and recommendations are specific.

## Our core themes

We will publish across education and human capital, economy and development, technology and opportunity, and governance and public policy.

This is also a living publication. As the Foundation grows, the archive can grow with it — with articles, research notes, explainers, interviews and data-led commentary collected in one permanent place.

## A note on sources

Where articles rely on reports, academic work, government documents or news reporting, references should be listed at the end of the article so readers can verify the evidence for themselves.

We hope this space becomes a useful record of ideas for Meghalaya — written with curiosity, care and a commitment to constructive public discussion.
